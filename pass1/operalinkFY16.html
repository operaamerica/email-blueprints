NEA FUNDING

Last week, the U.S. House of Representatives’ consideration of the Interior Appropriations bill, which includes funding the National Endowment for the Arts at $146 million for fiscal year 2016, stalled out. Amendments over the use of the Confederate flag resulted in the House pulling the Interior bill from consideration. Read more…

ESEA REAUTHORIZATION

The Senate continues to consider the Every Child Achieves Act (S. 1177), a bill to reauthorize the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA), also known as No Child Left Behind. The Senate bill retains the arts in the definition of core academic subjects, and an amendment is expected that would urge states to report on a number of indicators that demonstrate access to education, including access to core subjects of learning such as arts education. Read more…

OVERTIME PROPOSAL

On July 6, the U.S. Department of Labor announced a proposed rule that would increase the pay threshold to make more employees eligible for overtime compensation. Currently, employees who earn less than $23,660 per year, regardless of title or exempt classification, are eligible for overtime if they work more than 40 hours per week. Read more…
